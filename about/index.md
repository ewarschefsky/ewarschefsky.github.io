A native Michigander, I was lucky enough to grow up in a rural town where I was able to spend a great deal of time outdoors camping, canoeing, cross-country skiing and stargazing. I earned my B.A. in Biology from Reed College (Portland, OR) in 2009. As an undergraduate, I spent time exploring different fields of biology, from neuroscience to microbiology to ethnobotany before becoming passionate about studying plant evolution. While my research is focused on perennial crop domestication genetics and the evolution of crops and their wild relatives, my broader interests include speciation and the role of hybridization in speciation & evolution.
